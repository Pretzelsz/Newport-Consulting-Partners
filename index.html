About

Mr. Sweeney spent the first 22 years of his management consulting career with McKinsey & Company, the international top-management consulting firm, where he was a senior partner. During his years with McKinsey, he consulted with clients in the High Tech, Entertainment, Energy, Engineering & Construction, Health Care, and Internet Commerce industries. Leadership roles at McKinsey included the Global Internet practice, Southern California High Tech Practice, North American Recruiting and opening and leading McKinsey's Orange County office.

He is Chairman of the Board of Directors of Pharos Innovations, a Chicago-based health care services company serving patients with chronic disease conditions. He also serves on the boards of AdvoServ, a Delaware-based provider of clinical and residential services to individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities and challenging behavioral problems, and Plum Healthcare, a California-based provider of skilled nursing, rehabilitation, and broader post-acute services.

Mr. Sweeney received his undergraduate degree in economics from Princeton, and then served for four years in the U.S. Army as a Field Artillery officer, earning both Airborne and Ranger qualifications. He received his M.B.A., with distinction, from Harvard Business School. He is a cofounder and board chair for the Rising Tide Transitional Housing Program in Orange County, which provides housing and transition services for youth emancipating at age 18 from the foster care system. He also serves on the board of the South Coast region of Young Life, a non-denominational Christian youth organization. He is a member of the Chancellor's CEO Rountable at UC Irvine, where he chairs the Program Committee and serves as a member of the Steering Committee.
